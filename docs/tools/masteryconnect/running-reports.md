# Running Reports

MasteryConnect offers several report types that help you move from raw scores to instructional decisions. This guide covers the most useful reports for classroom teachers.

**Time to complete:** ~8 minutes

---

## Accessing reports

There are two ways to get to reports:

1. **From the tracker**: Click the **reporting icon** (:material-chart-bar:) next to any assessment in the Assessments tab.
2. **From the main menu**: Click **"Reports"** in the left sidebar for broader report options.

---

## Report types

### Item analysis

**What it shows**: How each question performed — which questions students got right, which they missed, and the most common wrong answers.

**Why it matters**: This tells you not just *that* students struggled, but *where exactly* the misunderstanding is.

**How to use it**:

1. Open the assessment in your tracker.
2. Click the **reporting icon** or select **"Item Analysis"**.
3. Review each question:
    - **High percentage correct** (80%+): Students understood this concept.
    - **Low percentage correct** (below 60%): This needs reteaching.
    - **Common wrong answer**: Look at which distractor students picked most. This reveals the specific misconception.

!!! tip "The wrong answer is the goldmine"
    Don't just look at the percentage correct. Look at **which wrong answer was most popular**. That tells you exactly what students misunderstood, and you can target your reteach to that specific misconception.

---

### Student mastery report

**What it shows**: An individual student's performance across all assessed standards.

**Why it matters**: Perfect for parent conferences, IEP meetings, and student goal-setting conversations.

**How to use it**:

1. Click on a **student's name** in your tracker.
2. Select **"Student Report"** or **"Mastery Report"**.
3. You'll see a breakdown of every standard with their mastery level.
4. You can **print** or **export** this report to share with parents or the student.

!!! info "One-click sharing"
    MasteryConnect lets you generate individual student reports with a single click — handy when you need reports for an entire class before conferences.

---

### Standards mastery report

**What it shows**: How the entire class performed on each standard — aggregated data across all assessments.

**Why it matters**: Helps you see the big picture of where your class stands across the curriculum.

**How to use it**:

1. From your tracker or the Reports menu, select **"Standards Report"**.
2. Review the percentage of students at mastery, near mastery, and remediation for each standard.
3. Sort by **lowest mastery** to identify your biggest instructional priorities.

---

### Assessment comparison

**What it shows**: How the same students performed across multiple assessments on the same standard over time.

**Why it matters**: Shows growth. Did reteaching work? Are students progressing?

**How to use it**:

1. From the Reports menu, select the assessment comparison option.
2. Choose two or more assessments on the same standard.
3. Compare the results side by side.

---

## Exporting and sharing reports

Most reports can be exported:

- **PDF**: Best for printing or emailing to parents
- **CSV**: Best for additional analysis in a spreadsheet

Look for the **export icon** (:material-download:) or **"Download"** button on any report screen.

---

## Best practices for reports

!!! tip "Tips from your ITRT"
    - **Run item analysis after every assessment** — even if the overall scores look good. The question-level data reveals what you'd otherwise miss.
    - **Use student reports for conferences** — showing parents a color-coded standards map is far more meaningful than a single grade.
    - **Bring standards reports to PLCs** — share your data with your team and compare across classrooms.
    - **Don't wait for summative data** — run reports on formative assessments to adjust instruction in real time.
