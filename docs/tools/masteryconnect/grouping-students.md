# Using Data to Group Students

The ultimate goal of MasteryConnect isn't just to collect data — it's to **act on it**. This guide shows you how to use your tracker and report data to form targeted instructional groups.

**Time to complete:** ~10 minutes

---

## Why group by mastery level?

Traditional grouping (high, medium, low) treats students as fixed categories. Mastery-based grouping is **standard-specific** — a student might be in the enrichment group for one standard and the intervention group for another. This is more targeted and more respectful of what students actually know.

---

## Step 1: Identify the standard to target

1. Open your **Mastery Tracker**.
2. Look for a standard with a **mix of colors** — some green, some yellow, some red.
3. This is your best candidate for small-group instruction because students have different needs.

!!! tip "If a whole column is red..."
    That's a whole-class reteach, not a small-group situation. Regroup after the reteach and re-assess.

---

## Step 2: Sort students by mastery level

From your tracker, identify three groups based on the color coding for your target standard:

### :red_circle: Remediation group (red)

These students did not demonstrate mastery and need **direct reteaching** of the concept.

**Instructional strategies**:

- Reteach using a different approach than the first time
- Use manipulatives, visuals, or worked examples
- Keep the group small (3–5 students if possible)
- Reassess after the intervention

### :yellow_circle: Near mastery group (yellow)

These students are close but have specific gaps. They need **targeted practice** rather than full reteaching.

**Instructional strategies**:

- Focus on the specific misconception (use your item analysis to identify it)
- Provide guided practice with immediate feedback
- Peer tutoring can work well for this group
- A single focused session may be enough to move them to green

### :green_circle: Mastery group (green)

These students have demonstrated proficiency. They need **enrichment or extension** — not more of the same.

**Instructional strategies**:

- Extension activities that deepen understanding
- Apply the standard to a new or more complex context
- Student-led projects or peer tutoring roles
- Independent work while you focus on the other groups

---

## Step 3: Plan your grouping session

Here's a practical structure for a class period using mastery-based groups:

| Time | Remediation Group | Near Mastery Group | Mastery Group |
|------|-------------------|--------------------|---------------|
| **First 10 min** | Direct instruction with teacher | Guided practice (task cards or partner work) | Independent extension activity |
| **Next 10 min** | Guided practice with teacher | Peer discussion and self-check | Choice activity or peer tutoring |
| **Last 5 min** | Quick exit ticket (3–5 questions) | Quick exit ticket | Share or present extension work |

!!! info "You don't have to do this every day"
    Mastery-based grouping is a strategy to use when the data shows a clear split. It's not meant to replace your regular instruction flow — it's a tool for the right moment.

---

## Step 4: Reassess and regroup

After your intervention:

1. Give a **short reassessment** (3–5 questions) on the same standard.
2. Administer it through MasteryConnect so the data flows back into your tracker.
3. Check the tracker — did students move from red/yellow to green?
4. **Regroup as needed** — students who are now green move on. Students still in red may need a different approach.

---

## Using this in PLC conversations

Mastery-based grouping is even more powerful when done **across classrooms**:

1. Compare tracker data with your team.
2. Identify which teachers have high mastery rates on specific standards.
3. Consider **cross-classroom grouping**: students rotate to the teacher whose instruction was most effective on that standard.
4. Reassess together and celebrate growth.

!!! tip "This is what data-driven PLCs look like"
    When your PLC conversation starts with tracker data and ends with a grouping plan, you're doing exactly what MasteryConnect was designed to support.
