# Using the Item Bank

The MasteryConnect item bank is a library of pre-built, standards-aligned assessment questions. It includes questions created by MasteryConnect, shared by other educators, and items authored by SCPS teachers. Learning to navigate the bank efficiently will save you hours of assessment creation time.

**Time to complete:** ~8 minutes

---

## Accessing the item bank

There are two ways to get to the item bank:

=== "From an assessment"
    While creating or editing an assessment, click **"Add Items from Bank"**. This filters the bank to standards already selected for your assessment.

=== "From the main menu"
    Click **"Item Bank"** in the left sidebar to browse all available items across all standards.

---

## Understanding item sources

Items in the bank come from different sources, and it helps to know which is which:

| Source | What It Means |
|--------|---------------|
| **Mastery View** | Created by MasteryConnect/Instructure. Generally high quality and well-aligned. |
| **SCPS Teacher Bank** | Created by teachers in Shenandoah County. Relevant to our pacing and curriculum. |
| **Personal Bank** | Items you've authored yourself. Only visible to you unless shared. |
| **Community** | Shared by educators nationwide. Quality varies — always preview before using. |

!!! tip "Start with Mastery View and SCPS"
    For the most reliable, well-aligned items, filter by **Mastery View** items first, then check the **SCPS Teacher Bank**. Community items can be useful but always preview them carefully.

## Filtering items effectively

The power of the item bank is in the filters. Here's how to use them:

### Standard

- Select the specific Virginia SOL standard you're assessing.
- Be as specific as possible — choosing a broad strand will return hundreds of results.

### Question type

| Type | Best For |
|------|----------|
| **Multiple Choice** | Quick formative checks, auto-scored |
| **True/False** | Quick recall checks, auto-scored |
| **Multi-Select** | Deeper understanding (select all that apply), auto-scored |
| **Short Answer** | Checking student reasoning, requires manual scoring |
| **Essay/Extended Response** | Performance-based assessment, requires manual scoring |

### Difficulty and DOK level

- **DOK 1 (Recall)**: Can the student remember the fact or definition?
- **DOK 2 (Skill/Concept)**: Can the student apply the concept?
- **DOK 3 (Strategic Thinking)**: Can the student reason and plan?

!!! info "Mix your DOK levels"
    A good formative assessment includes a mix — a few DOK 1 items to check baseline recall, and DOK 2 items to see if students can apply what they've learned.

### Language

Make sure **English** is selected unless you're intentionally looking for translated items.

## Previewing and selecting items

1. Click on any item to **preview** the full question, answer choices, and correct answer.
2. Check that the item is:
    - **Accurately aligned** to the standard you're assessing
    - **Clearly worded** and free of errors
    - **Appropriate** for your students' reading level
3. If the item looks good, check the box to **select it**.
4. When you've selected all the items you want, click **"Add Selected"** to add them to your assessment.

---

## Authoring your own items

If you can't find what you need in the bank, you can create your own items that will be saved to your personal bank and the SCPS teacher bank.

1. From the item bank or assessment builder, click **"+ Create Item"**.
2. Choose the question type.
3. Write the question stem and answer choices.
4. Mark the correct answer.
5. Align it to a standard.
6. Click **"Save"**.

!!! tip "Help your colleagues"
    Every item you author goes into the SCPS teacher bank. Write clear, well-aligned questions and your teammates can use them too. Consider collaborating with your PLC to build a shared bank for your course.

---

## Common pitfalls

!!! warning "Watch out for these"
    - **Not previewing items**: Some community items have errors or poor alignment. Always preview.
    - **Too many passage-based items in ELA**: Five questions with five different passages creates an unintentionally long assessment. Pick 1–2 passages and pull multiple questions from each.
    - **Ignoring DOK levels**: An assessment with all DOK 1 items won't tell you much about student understanding.
    - **Forgetting to check the answer key**: Occasionally items in the community bank have incorrect answer keys. Verify before administering.
