# Creating an Assessment

Assessments in MasteryConnect are aligned to specific standards, giving you targeted data on what students know and can do. This guide covers creating an assessment from scratch and from the item bank.

**Time to complete:** ~10 minutes

---

## Before you begin

!!! warning "Prerequisites"
    - Your Mastery Tracker is set up with standards (see [Setting up your Mastery Tracker](setup-tracker.md))
    - Your tracker is linked to Canvas (see [Linking to Canvas](link-to-canvas.md))

---

## Option A: Create an assessment from the item bank

This is the fastest way to build an assessment using pre-made, standards-aligned questions.

### Step 1: Open the assessment builder

1. From your Mastery Tracker, click the **"Assessments"** tab.
2. Click **"+ Create Assessment"**.
3. Give your assessment a **clear name** (e.g., `Unit 3 - Fractions Check`).

### Step 2: Select your standard(s)

1. Choose the **standard(s)** this assessment will measure.
2. You can assess one standard or multiple — but keep it focused for formative checks.

!!! tip "Keep it tight"
    For formative assessments, focus on **1–2 standards** with **5–10 questions**. This gives you actionable data without overwhelming students.

### Step 3: Browse the item bank

1. Click **"Add Items from Bank"** or **"Browse Items"**.
2. Filter items using these options:

    | Filter | Recommendation |
    |--------|----------------|
    | **Standard** | Should auto-filter based on your selection |
    | **Question Type** | Multiple choice and true/false are fastest to score |
    | **DOK Level** | Mix DOK 1 (recall) and DOK 2 (application) for balanced data |
    | **Difficulty** | Start with medium difficulty for a baseline |

3. **Preview each item** before adding it — click the item to see the full question.
4. Check the box next to items you want, then click **"Add Selected"**.

!!! info "ELA teachers: a note about passages"
    If you're selecting items with reading passages, be intentional. Each passage adds reading time. A 5-question assessment with 5 different passages will take much longer than 5 questions on a single passage.

### Step 4: Review and finalize

1. Review the assessment in the builder. You can **reorder questions** by dragging them.
2. Check that the **answer key** is correct for each item.
3. Click **"Create Assessment"** to save it to your tracker.

## Option B: Create an assessment from scratch

If you want to write your own questions or upload a PDF, use this method.

### Step 1: Start a new assessment

1. From the **Assessments** tab, click **"+ Create Assessment"**.
2. Name the assessment and select the standard(s).
3. Choose **"Author Items"** instead of browsing the bank.

### Step 2: Add questions

1. Click **"+ Add Question"**.
2. Select the **question type**:
    - **Multiple Choice** — most common, auto-scored
    - **True/False** — quick checks
    - **Short Answer** — requires manual scoring
    - **Performance Task** — rubric-based, for deeper assessment

3. Type your question and answer choices.
4. **Mark the correct answer** by clicking on it.
5. Align the question to a standard if it isn't already.

### Step 3: Upload a PDF (alternative)

If you have an existing assessment as a document:

1. Choose **"Upload PDF"** in the assessment builder.
2. Upload your file.
3. Set the **number and type of questions**.
4. **Enter the answer key** manually.

!!! warning "Don't include the answer key in your PDF"
    If you upload a PDF, make sure the answer key is **not** part of the document. Students will see the PDF directly.

### Step 4: Finalize

1. Review all questions and the answer key.
2. Choose whether to make the assessment **printable** (enables bubble sheet scoring).
3. Click **"Create Assessment"**.

---

## After creating your assessment

Your assessment is now saved to your tracker but is in **draft mode** until you administer it.

!!! info "Draft vs. Created"
    - **Draft**: Only you can see it. You can still edit questions and answers.
    - **Created**: Locked in. Students can access it. You can still view reports after scoring.

---

## Best practices

!!! tip "Tips from your ITRT"
    - **Name assessments consistently**: `Unit # - Topic - Type` (e.g., `Unit 4 - Photosynthesis - Formative`)
    - **Limit standards per assessment**: 1–2 for formative, 3–5 for summative
    - **Preview as a student**: Always preview your assessment before administering
    - **Share with your team**: Items you author go into the SCPS teacher bank — your colleagues can benefit from your work
    - **Check question types**: Only multiple choice and true/false are auto-scored. Short answer and performance tasks require manual grading.
