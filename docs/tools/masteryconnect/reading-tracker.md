# Reading the Mastery Tracker

The Mastery Tracker is a color-coded grid that shows you, at a glance, where every student stands on every standard. Understanding how to read it is essential for using MasteryConnect effectively.

**Time to complete:** ~8 minutes

---

## The tracker layout

The Mastery Tracker is organized as a **grid**:

- **Rows** = Students (listed down the left side)
- **Columns** = Standards (listed across the top)
- **Cells** = Each cell represents one student's mastery level on one standard

---

## Understanding the colors

Each cell in the tracker is color-coded based on the student's performance:

| Color | Meaning | What It Tells You |
|-------|---------|-------------------|
| :green_circle: **Green** | Mastery | Student has demonstrated proficiency on this standard |
| :yellow_circle: **Yellow** | Near Mastery | Student is approaching proficiency but not there yet |
| :red_circle: **Red** | Remediation | Student has not demonstrated proficiency and needs intervention |
| :white_circle: **Gray/Empty** | No Data | No assessment has been scored for this standard yet |

!!! info "How are colors determined?"
    The color is based on the **cut scores** set for your tracker. By default:

    - **Green (Mastery)**: 80%+ correct
    - **Yellow (Near Mastery)**: 60–79% correct
    - **Red (Remediation)**: Below 60% correct

    These thresholds may be adjusted by your school or district. Check with your ITRT if you're unsure of your current settings.

---

## Reading patterns in your data

The real power of the tracker is spotting **patterns** across students and standards.

### Pattern 1: A whole column is red

If an entire standard column is red or mostly red, it means **most of your class struggled with that standard**. This is an instructional signal — you likely need to reteach this concept to the whole group.

### Pattern 2: A whole row is red

If one student's entire row is red, that student is **struggling broadly** and may need intensive intervention beyond just one standard.

### Pattern 3: Mixed colors across a standard

If a standard column shows a mix of green, yellow, and red, that's a signal for **differentiated instruction** — some students are ready to move on while others need support.

### Pattern 4: Green everywhere except one standard

If a student is green on everything except one standard, that's a **targeted intervention opportunity**. A quick reteach or small group session on that one standard could move them to mastery.

## Tracker views

MasteryConnect offers different ways to view your tracker data:

=== "Standards View (Default)"
    The standard grid view with students as rows and standards as columns. Best for day-to-day monitoring.

=== "Assessment List View"
    Shows assessments grouped by standard. You can see which assessments are in draft or created status, how many students have completed each one, and access reports directly. Toggle to this with the **"Assessments"** tab.

=== "Student View"
    Click on any student's name to see their individual mastery profile across all standards. Great for parent conferences and student goal-setting conversations.

## What the tracker shows (and doesn't show)

!!! tip "Important to know"
    - The tracker displays results from the **most recent assessment** for each standard by default. It does not average all assessments together.
    - If you've given multiple assessments on the same standard, the most recent one determines the color.
    - To see historical performance, use the **reports** feature (see [Running reports](running-reports.md)).

## Using the tracker in PLCs and team meetings

The Mastery Tracker is a powerful tool for collaborative conversations:

1. **Identify common gaps**: Which standards are red across multiple teachers' trackers?
2. **Share strategies**: If one teacher's tracker is green on a standard others are struggling with, that teacher may have effective strategies to share.
3. **Plan interventions**: Use the data to form small groups across classrooms.
