# Diffit Tips and Best Practices

Strategies for using Diffit effectively across subjects, plus common pitfalls to avoid.

**Time to complete:** ~5 min

---

## Subject-specific strategies

### ELA

- Use Diffit to create **leveled versions of the same text** so all students can participate in the same discussion
- Generate **vocabulary lists** from your current unit texts
- Create **comprehension questions at multiple DOK levels** to differentiate assessment

### Science

- Paste a complex scientific article and have Diffit **simplify it** for your grade level
- Use **YouTube videos** of experiments or phenomena as input — Diffit turns them into readable passages
- Generate **vocabulary lists** for unit-specific scientific terminology

### Social Studies

- Input primary source documents and have Diffit create **accessible versions** while preserving key historical language
- Create **multiple reading levels** of the same historical event for differentiated stations
- Use the **discussion prompts** for Socratic seminar preparation

### Math

- Use Diffit for the **word problem reading** component — create leveled versions of word problems where the math stays the same but the reading complexity changes
- Generate **vocabulary lists** for mathematical terms

### ELL / Multilingual Learners

- Generate passages in **multiple languages** — Diffit supports English, Spanish, French, and more
- Create **bilingual vocabulary lists** with definitions in both languages
- Use **lower reading level** passages to give ELL students access to grade-level content

---

## Common mistakes to avoid

!!! warning "Watch out for these"
    - **Not reviewing the content**: Diffit uses AI, and AI makes mistakes. Always read through generated passages for factual accuracy.
    - **Using only one reading level**: The whole point is differentiation. Create at least 2–3 levels for each passage to serve your full range of learners.
    - **Ignoring the vocabulary list**: The auto-generated vocabulary is one of Diffit's most useful features. Use it for pre-teaching, word walls, or study guides.
    - **Forgetting to align to standards**: Diffit lets you select standards. Use this feature to ensure your materials are aligned to Virginia SOLs.
    - **Replacing your own materials entirely**: Diffit supplements your instruction — it doesn't replace your expertise, your texts, or your knowledge of your students.

|--------------|---------|
| Create a leveled reading | Choose topic or paste text → select grade level → Generate |
| Make it easier for a struggling reader | Regenerate at a lower grade level |
| Get comprehension questions | They're auto-generated with every passage |
| Create a vocabulary study tool | Export the vocabulary list to Google Docs or PDF |
| Make an auto-graded quiz | Export questions to Google Forms |
| Assign to students | Export to Google Classroom |
| Create from a video | Paste a YouTube URL as your input |
| Support ELL students | Generate in their home language or at a lower reading level |
