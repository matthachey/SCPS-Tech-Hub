# Generating Differentiated Reading Passages

The core power of Diffit is creating reading passages at different levels from a single source. This guide covers how to create leveled texts for your classroom.

**Time to complete:** ~8 min

---

## Creating a passage from a topic

1. From the Diffit dashboard, choose **"Topic"** as your input.
2. Enter the topic (e.g., "The water cycle").
3. Select the **grade level** for the passage.
4. Click **"Generate"**.
5. Diffit creates a reading passage at that level with key vocabulary highlighted.

### Creating multiple versions

To differentiate for your classroom, generate the same topic at **multiple reading levels**:

- Create one version at your grade level (e.g., 7th grade)
- Create another at a lower level for struggling readers or ELL students (e.g., 4th grade)
- Create a higher version for advanced readers (e.g., 9th grade)

All three versions cover the **same core content** — students discuss the same topic but read at their level.

!!! tip "Same content, different levels"
    This is the magic of Diffit. A student reading at a 4th-grade level and a student reading at a 9th-grade level can participate in the same class discussion about the same topic. Neither student is excluded.

---

## Creating a passage from an article or video

### From a web article

1. Select **"Article"** or paste a URL.
2. Diffit reads the article and rewrites it at your chosen reading level.
3. The core facts and ideas stay the same — only the language complexity changes.

### From a YouTube video

1. Select **"YouTube video"** and paste the video URL.
2. Diffit transcribes and converts the video content into a reading passage.
3. Choose the reading level and generate.

!!! tip "Turn videos into readings"
    This is incredibly useful. If you show a video in class but want students to have a text to reference later, Diffit can create one from the video in seconds.

---

## Refining your sources

Diffit lets you refine which sources inform the generated readings. You can:

- **Select or deselect sources** that Diffit finds
- **Add your own context** to guide the content
- **Adjust standards alignment** to target specific learning objectives
- **Tune vocabulary** to include or exclude specific terms
