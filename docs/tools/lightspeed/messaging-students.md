# Sending Messages to Students

Lightspeed lets you send instant messages directly to student devices — a quiet, non-disruptive way to redirect behavior, check in, or make announcements.

**Time to complete:** ~5 minutes

---

## Sending a message to the whole class

1. From your class view, click the **"Message"** button in the top toolbar.
2. Make sure **"Entire Class"** is selected.
3. Type your message or choose from a **predefined message** (e.g., "Are you done?" or "Need help?").
4. Click **"Send"**.

The message appears as a **notification on every student's screen**. Students can respond with **"Yes"** or **"No"**.

---

## Sending a message to one student

1. Click on the **student's thumbnail** to open their individual view.
2. Click the **"Message"** icon.
3. Type a custom message or select a predefined one.
4. Click **"Send"**.

!!! tip "The quiet redirect"
    This is one of the most powerful features in Lightspeed. Instead of calling out a student in front of the class, send a quiet message: "Hey — I see you're not on the assignment. Need help?" It preserves the student's dignity and keeps class moving.

---

## Sending a message to a group

If you've created student groups (see [Creating student groups](student-groups.md)):

1. Select the **group** from the left sidebar.
2. Click **"Message"**.
3. Your message will go to only the students in that group.

---

## Predefined messages

Lightspeed includes predefined messages you can send with one click. Common options include:

- "Are you done?"
- "Need help?"
- "Please stay on task"
- "Eyes up here"

These are great for quick check-ins without needing to type.

!!! info "Student responses"
    When a student receives a message, they see it as a browser notification and can tap **"Yes"** or **"No"**. Their response shows up on your dashboard next to their name.

---

## Best practices

!!! tip "Tips from your ITRT"
    - **Use messages before locking screens or closing tabs.** Give the student a chance to self-correct first.
    - **Keep messages short and kind.** "Need help getting started?" works better than "Get on task."
    - **Use predefined messages for speed.** During a busy class, one-tap messages save time.
    - **Check for responses.** If a student says "Yes" to "Need help?", follow up in person.
