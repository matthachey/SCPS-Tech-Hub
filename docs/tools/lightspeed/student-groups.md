# Creating Student Groups with Custom Rules

Student groups in Lightspeed let you apply different web rules, push different links, and send messages to subsets of your class. This is perfect for differentiated instruction, group projects, or tiered interventions.

**Time to complete:** ~8 minutes

---

## Why use groups?

Without groups, every web rule you set applies to the **entire class**. With groups, you can:

- Give your research group access to the internet while your testing group is locked to one site
- Push different links to different groups working on different activities
- Send targeted messages to just the students who need a redirect

---

## Creating a group

1. Open your class in Lightspeed.
2. Hover over the space under **"My Class"** on the left sidebar.
3. Click **"Create Group"**.
4. Name the group (e.g., `Table 1`, `Research Group`, `Intervention`, `Testing`).
5. Click **"Save"**.

Your new group appears in the left sidebar below "My Class."

---

## Adding students to a group

1. From the main class view, find the student you want to add.
2. **Drag and drop** the student's tile from "My Class" into the group on the left sidebar.
3. Repeat for all students in that group.

You can also drag students **between groups** or **back to My Class** to reorganize.

!!! tip "Quick setup"
    Set up your groups at the beginning of the period before students start working. It takes 30 seconds to drag a few students into groups, and it saves you time switching rules later.

---

## Setting group-specific web rules

Each group has its own **Group Web Rules** that work independently from the class rules.

1. Click on the **group name** in the left sidebar to select it.
2. Click **"Group Web Rules"** (or look for the web rules option within the group).
3. Set up **Allow** or **Block** lists just for that group (see [Allow and block lists](allow-block-lists.md) for details).
4. Apply the list.

### Example: Two groups, two sets of rules

| Group | Activity | Web Rule |
|-------|----------|----------|
| **Research Group** | Working on a research paper | Allow Mode: `docs.google.com`, `scholar.google.com`, `britannica.com` |
| **Testing Group** | Taking a quiz | Allow Mode: `forms.google.com` only |

Students in each group only see the sites allowed for their group.

---

## Group-specific actions

With a group selected, you can also:

- **Push a link** to just that group
- **Send a message** to just that group
- **Lock screens** for just that group
- **Share your screen** to just that group

This makes it easy to run multiple activities simultaneously.

---

## Removing groups

When you no longer need a group:

1. Drag all students back to **"My Class"**.
2. The empty group can be deleted or left for reuse next time.

!!! info "Groups reset between sessions"
    Groups you create are tied to the class, but you may need to re-add students each session depending on your setup. Some teachers create their standard groups at the start of each week.

---

## Best practices

!!! tip "Tips from your ITRT"
    - **Name groups clearly**: `Table 1` or `Red Group` is fine for quick grouping. `Intervention - Standard 3.2` is better for targeted instruction.
    - **Use groups for MTSS**: If you're running tiered interventions, groups let you give Tier 2 students access to specific practice sites while Tier 1 students work independently.
    - **Combine with Push Link**: Push the assignment link to each group separately so each group lands exactly where they need to be.
