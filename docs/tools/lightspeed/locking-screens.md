# Locking Screens and Pausing Internet

Sometimes you need every eye off a screen and on you. Lightspeed lets you lock student screens or disable internet access with one click.

**Time to complete:** ~3 minutes

---

## Locking all screens

1. From your class view, click the **"Lock Screens"** button in the top toolbar.
2. Every student's device displays a **lock screen message** — they cannot interact with their device.
3. Click **"Unlock Screens"** when you're ready for them to resume.

### When to lock screens

| Scenario | Why It Works |
|----------|--------------|
| Giving instructions | No distractions — every student sees the lock screen |
| Transitioning between activities | Students close laptops mentally when screens are locked |
| Class discussion | Removes the temptation to multitask |
| Taking attendance or making announcements | Quick way to get attention |

---

## Locking one student's screen

1. Click on the **student's thumbnail**.
2. Click the **"Lock"** button in their individual view.
3. Only that student's device is locked — the rest of the class continues working.
4. Click **"Unlock"** when you're ready.

!!! tip "The individual lock"
    This is a great alternative to taking a device away. If a student is persistently off-task, lock their screen for a few minutes while you have a quiet conversation with them. It's less disruptive than collecting the Chromebook.

---

## Closing off-task tabs

Instead of locking the whole screen, you can surgically close specific tabs:

1. Click on a student's thumbnail to see their open tabs.
2. Find the off-task tab.
3. Click the **"X"** or **"Close"** button next to that tab.
4. The tab closes on the student's device.

The student keeps working on their other tabs — you just removed the distraction.

---

## Disabling internet access

Lightspeed can also fully disable internet access for a student or the whole class:

1. Look for the **"Disable Internet"** or **"Pause Internet"** option.
2. Toggle it on for the class or individual students.
3. Students' devices remain on but cannot access any websites.
4. Toggle it off to restore access.

!!! info "Lock screens vs. disable internet"
    - **Lock screens**: The student sees a lock message and can't do anything on the device.
    - **Disable internet**: The student can still use offline apps and documents but can't browse the web.

    Choose based on whether you want them completely stopped (lock) or just offline (disable internet).

---

## Best practices

!!! tip "Tips from your ITRT"
    - **Give a verbal heads-up** before locking screens: "Screens are going to lock in 10 seconds — save your work." This prevents lost work and frustrated students.
    - **Use lock screens briefly** — 2–3 minutes for instructions, then unlock. Extended locking can feel punitive.
    - **Close tabs before escalating to a lock.** Give the student a chance to self-correct.
    - **Pair with screen sharing.** Lock screens → share your screen → demonstrate → unlock screens → students work.
