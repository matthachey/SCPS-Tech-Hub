# Viewing Student Screens

Real-time screen monitoring is the core feature of Lightspeed Classroom. This guide covers how to view the whole class, zoom into individual students, and check browsing history.

**Time to complete:** ~5 minutes

---

## Viewing the whole class

When you open an active class, you'll see a **grid of student screen thumbnails** — small live previews of what every student is currently seeing on their device.

### Adjust the view

Use the **zoom slider** to adjust how many screens you see at once:

- **Zoomed out**: See all students at a glance — great for a quick scan of the room
- **Zoomed in**: See fewer students but with larger, more readable thumbnails

!!! tip "The quick scan"
    At the start of class, zoom out to see everyone. Quickly scan for any screens that look obviously off-task (games, YouTube, social media). Then zoom in on those specific students.

---

## Viewing an individual student

Click on any student's thumbnail to zoom into their screen. From the individual student view, you can:

- **See their full screen** in real time (on Windows/Mac, this shows the entire desktop, not just the browser)
- **View their open tabs** listed below the screen
- **Check their browsing history** for the class period
- **Send them a message** directly
- **Close specific tabs** that are off-task
- **Lock their screen** individually

### Checking browsing history

1. Click on a student's name or thumbnail.
2. Select **"History"** to see every site they've visited during the current class session.
3. This is useful for verifying whether a student was on task even when you weren't watching.

---

## Understanding screen thumbnail indicators

Not every student's screen will display a clear thumbnail. Here's what you might see:

| What You See | What It Means |
|--------------|---------------|
| Clear screen thumbnail | Student is online and being monitored normally |
| "Thumbnail unavailable" | Student has a new blank tab open or is on a page that can't be captured |
| Grayed out / offline icon | Student's device is not connected |
| Screen recording icon | You are recording this student's screen |

!!! info "Chromebook vs. Windows/Mac"
    On **Chromebooks**, Lightspeed can only see the browser window. On **Windows and Mac devices**, Lightspeed can see the full desktop — including other applications. This means Chromebook students could potentially use Android apps without being seen in Lightspeed.

---

## Recording a student's screen

If a student is persistently off-task and you need documentation:

1. Click on the student's thumbnail to open their individual view.
2. Click the **"Record"** button.
3. The screen will be recorded for the duration of the class session (or until you stop it).
4. The recording is saved and can be shared with parents or administration.

!!! warning "Privacy note"
    Screen recording is limited to the **active class session only**. Recordings are meant for documenting off-task behavior for parent/admin conversations. Use this feature thoughtfully and professionally.

---

## Best practices for screen monitoring

!!! tip "Tips from your ITRT"
    - **Don't sit at your desk and stare at Lightspeed all period.** Use it as a tool, not a replacement for walking the room. A quick glance every few minutes is enough.
    - **Let students know you can see their screens.** The awareness alone reduces off-task behavior dramatically.
    - **Use the zoom slider strategically.** Start zoomed out for a scan, then zoom in on students who need attention.
    - **Check history, not just live screens.** A student might close a tab before you see it live, but the history doesn't lie.
