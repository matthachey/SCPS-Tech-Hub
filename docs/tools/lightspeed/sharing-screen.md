# Sharing Your Screen

Screen sharing in Lightspeed broadcasts what's on your screen to all student devices — useful for demonstrations, walkthroughs, or getting everyone's attention.

**Time to complete:** ~3 minutes

---

## How to share your screen

1. From your class view, click the **"Share Screen"** button in the top toolbar.
2. Choose what to share (your entire screen or a specific browser tab, depending on your setup).
3. Click **"Start Sharing"**.
4. Students will see your screen displayed on their devices.
5. Click **"Stop Sharing"** when you're done.

---

## When to use screen sharing

| Scenario | How It Helps |
|----------|--------------|
| Walking through a website or tool | Students follow along on their own screens |
| Modeling an assignment | Show exactly what you expect |
| Reviewing as a class | Display a document or presentation |

!!! tip "Combine with Lock Screens"
    For maximum attention, **lock student screens** first (see [Locking screens](locking-screens.md)), then share your screen. This prevents students from clicking away while you're presenting.

---

## Student-to-class screen sharing

Lightspeed also allows you to broadcast a **student's screen** to the rest of the class — great for student presentations or sharing exemplary work.

1. Click on the student's thumbnail.
2. Select **"Share to Class"** (or similar option).
3. The student's screen is now visible to everyone.
4. Stop sharing when the student is finished.

!!! warning "Get permission first"
    Always ask the student before sharing their screen with the class. You don't want to accidentally broadcast something personal or embarrassing.
