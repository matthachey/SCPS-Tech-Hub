# Using the AccessScan Chrome Extension

The Lightspeed AccessScan extension lets you check whether a website or specific page is accessible to your students before you plan a lesson around it. It scans the page and shows you exactly which elements are allowed and which are blocked by the district content filter.

**Time to complete:** ~5 min

---

## What is AccessScan?

AccessScan is a **Chrome extension** pushed out to all SCPS teacher accounts. It lets you browse to any website, run a scan, and see — from a student's perspective — what's allowed and what's blocked.

This is different from your classroom Allow/Block lists in Lightspeed Classroom. AccessScan checks the **district-level content filter**, which applies to all students regardless of your class web rules.

!!! tip "Why this matters"
    Just because YOU can see a website doesn't mean your students can. Teacher and student filter settings are different. A site that loads fine on your account might be completely blocked for students. Always check with AccessScan before planning a lesson around a new website.

---

## How to use AccessScan

### Step 1: Navigate to the website

Open Chrome (signed into your SCPS Google account) and go to the website you want to check. For example: `https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_Revolution`

### Step 2: Open the AccessScan extension

1. Look for the **Lightspeed AccessScan icon** in your Chrome extension bar (top right of the browser — it looks like a light blue link/chain icon).
2. If you don't see it, click the **puzzle piece** icon to show all extensions and find AccessScan.
3. Click the AccessScan icon.

### Step 3: Enter a student's email

1. In the AccessScan popup, type a **student's email address** (e.g., `jsmith@shenandoah.k12.va.us`).
2. Click **"Scan Page"**.

### Step 4: Review the results

AccessScan will show you a list of every resource on that page and whether it's:

| Status | What It Means |
|--------|---------------|
| **Allowed** | Students can access this resource |
| **Blocked** | This resource is blocked by the district filter |

If any critical elements are blocked (the page itself, embedded videos, login redirects), your students won't have a good experience — even if the main URL appears accessible.

---

## What to do if a site is blocked

If AccessScan shows that a site you need is blocked:

1. Click **"Email results"** in the AccessScan window (if available).
2. AccessScan generates a summary of what's blocked.
3. Click **"Copy to Clipboard"**.
4. Open your email and paste the results into a new message.
5. Send the request to your building's IT support (follow your school's ticket process for filter requests).
6. Include the **URL**, your **reason for needing access**, and the **date you need it by**.

!!! tip "Plan ahead"
    Submit unblock requests **at least a few days** before you plan to use the site. Filter changes may need admin approval and won't happen instantly.

---

## Common scenarios

| Scenario | What to Do |
|----------|------------|
| Planning a lesson with a new website | Scan it first. If it's blocked, submit a request before building the lesson around it. |
| YouTube video is blocked | Check the specific video URL. Individual videos may be blocked even if YouTube itself is allowed. Consider creating a YouTube playlist and sharing it with IT for bulk unblocking. |
| Embedded content doesn't load for students | The main site may be allowed but embedded resources (fonts, scripts, CDNs) might be blocked. Scan the page to see all resources. |
| Site works on your teacher device but not student devices | Your teacher filter settings are more permissive. AccessScan shows you the student experience. |

---

## Important notes

!!! info "AccessScan vs. your Lightspeed Classroom web rules"
    - **AccessScan** checks the **district-level content filter** — the baseline filter that applies to all students everywhere.
    - **Lightspeed Classroom Allow/Block lists** are your **class-level rules** that layer on top of the district filter.
    - Even if you allow a site in your Lightspeed Classroom Allow List, it will still be blocked if the district filter blocks it. AccessScan helps you identify these conflicts.
