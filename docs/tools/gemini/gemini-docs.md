# Gemini in Google Docs

Gemini in Docs acts as a writing partner — it can draft content from a prompt, rewrite existing text, summarize long documents, and help you get past a blank page.

**Time to complete:** ~8 min

---

## "Help me write" — drafting from scratch

1. Open a **Google Doc**.
2. Click the **Gemini sparkle icon** in the toolbar (or look for "Help me write" in the left margin).
3. Type a prompt describing what you want. Examples:
    - "Write a parent newsletter about our upcoming field trip to Luray Caverns"
    - "Draft a lesson plan for teaching the water cycle to 6th graders"
    - "Create a letter of recommendation template for a high school student"
4. Click **"Create"**.
5. Gemini generates a draft. You can:
    - **Insert** it into your document
    - **Regenerate** for a different version
    - **Refine** by asking Gemini to make it shorter, more formal, more detailed, etc.

---

## Rewriting existing text

1. **Select text** in your document that you want to improve.
2. Click the **Gemini icon** that appears.
3. Choose an option:
    - **Shorten** — make it more concise
    - **Elaborate** — add more detail
    - **Rephrase** — reword for clarity
    - **More formal / casual** — adjust the tone
4. Review the suggestion and accept or modify it.

---

## Summarizing documents

If you have a long document (meeting notes, report, article):

1. Open the **Gemini side panel** (click the Gemini icon in the right sidebar).
2. Type: "Summarize this document" or "What are the key points?"
3. Gemini reads the document and provides a summary.

This is especially useful for quickly getting up to speed on long documents shared by your team or administration.

---

## Practical use cases for teachers

| Task | Prompt Example |
|------|----------------|
| Parent communication | "Write a brief email to parents about our class poetry reading on Friday" |
| Lesson planning | "Create a 5E lesson plan on photosynthesis for 9th grade biology" |
| IEP meeting prep | "Summarize this student progress document into key talking points" |
| Rubric creation | "Create a 4-point rubric for a persuasive essay" |
| Sub plans | "Draft substitute teacher plans for a day in my 7th grade math class covering fractions" |

!!! warning "Always review and edit"
    Gemini drafts are starting points, not finished products. Always review for accuracy, tone, and alignment with your specific classroom context before sharing.
