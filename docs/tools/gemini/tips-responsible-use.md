# Gemini Tips and Responsible Use

Getting the most out of Gemini requires good prompting skills and an understanding of its limitations. This guide covers both.

**Time to complete:** ~5 min

---

## Writing effective prompts

The quality of Gemini's output depends entirely on the quality of your prompt. Here's a framework:

### Be specific

| Vague Prompt | Better Prompt |
|-------------|---------------|
| "Write a lesson plan" | "Write a 45-minute lesson plan on figurative language for 7th grade ELA, including a bell ringer, direct instruction, guided practice, and an exit ticket" |
| "Make a quiz" | "Create a 10-question multiple choice quiz on the water cycle for 5th graders, with DOK levels 1 and 2" |
| "Help me write an email" | "Write a professional email to parents informing them about the upcoming science fair, including the date (March 21), what students need to prepare, and a request for volunteers" |

### Include context

Tell Gemini who the audience is, what grade level, what subject, and any constraints:

- "For 9th grade biology students..."
- "In language appropriate for ELL students at intermediate proficiency..."
- "Following Virginia SOL standards for US History..."

### Iterate

Don't accept the first draft. Follow up with:

- "Make it shorter"
- "Add more examples"
- "Adjust for a lower reading level"
- "Make the tone more encouraging"
- "Add a rubric"

---

## Limitations to understand

!!! warning "Gemini is not perfect"
    - **It can generate inaccurate information.** Always fact-check, especially for historical dates, scientific details, and specific data.
    - **It doesn't know your students.** You know your classroom context, student needs, and school culture better than any AI.
    - **It may not align perfectly with Virginia SOLs.** Always verify standards alignment against the actual SOL framework.
    - **It can sound generic.** Add your personal voice and specific classroom references to make content feel authentic.

---

## Responsible use guidelines

### For teachers

- **Use Gemini as a starting point**, not a finished product
- **Review everything** before sharing with students, parents, or administration
- **Don't enter student PII** (names, IDs, grades, IEP details) into Gemini prompts
- **Be transparent** — if asked, be honest that you used AI to help draft content
- **Model good AI use** for students — show them how you use it as a tool, not a replacement for thinking

### For student-facing content

- Always **verify accuracy** of any facts, dates, or data in generated content
- **Edit for your classroom context** — add references to class discussions, local examples, and student interests
- **Check reading level** — Gemini's estimate of reading level may not match your assessment

## Quick reference: Prompt templates

| Task | Prompt Template |
|------|----------------|
| Lesson plan | "Create a [time] lesson plan on [topic] for [grade/subject], including [components]. Align to Virginia SOL [standard]." |
| Parent email | "Write a [tone] email to parents about [topic], including [key details]." |
| Quiz | "Create [#] [question types] questions on [topic] for [grade], at DOK levels [1/2/3]." |
| Differentiated text | "Rewrite this passage at a [grade] reading level, keeping the key concepts: [paste text]" |
| Rubric | "Create a [#]-point rubric for [assignment type] assessing [criteria]." |
| Discussion prompt | "Write [#] discussion questions about [topic] that encourage critical thinking for [grade]." |
