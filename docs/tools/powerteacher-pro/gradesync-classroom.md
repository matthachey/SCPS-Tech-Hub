# Syncing Grades from Google Classroom

If you use Google Classroom for assignments, you can export grades to PowerTeacher Pro so they appear on report cards and the parent portal.

**Time to complete:** ~8 min

---

## How the integration works

Google Classroom integrates with PowerSchool SIS, allowing teachers to:

- **Export grades** from Google Classroom to PowerTeacher Pro
- **Import rosters** and grading categories from PowerSchool into Classroom

!!! info "Admin setup required"
    The Google Classroom–PowerSchool connection must be set up by your SCPS admin. If you don't see the SIS export option in Google Classroom, check with your ITRT.

---

## Exporting grades from Google Classroom

1. Open your class in **Google Classroom**.
2. Go to the **Grades** tab.
3. Look for the **SIS export** option (this appears if the integration is enabled).
4. Select the assignments you want to export.
5. Map your Google Classroom **grade categories** to the corresponding **PowerTeacher Pro categories**.
6. Click **Export**.

Grades will transfer to PowerTeacher Pro for the mapped assignments.

---

## Alternative: Manual CSV transfer

If the direct integration isn't enabled, you can transfer grades manually:

1. In Google Classroom, go to the **Grades** tab.
2. Click the **gear icon** → **"Copy all grades to Google Sheets"**.
3. Open the Google Sheet and download it as a **CSV file**.
4. In PowerTeacher Pro, go to your course → click the **gear icon** → **"Import Scores"**.
5. Upload the CSV file and map the columns to match your students and assignments.

!!! tip "Check student matching"
    When importing, make sure student names or IDs in the CSV match what PowerTeacher Pro expects. Mismatches will cause grades to land on the wrong students or fail to import.

---

## Best practices

!!! tip "Tips from your ITRT"
    - **Pick one primary gradebook**: If you're grading in Google Classroom, consistently export to PowerTeacher Pro. Don't split some grades in Classroom and others directly in PowerTeacher — it creates confusion.
    - **Export regularly**: Don't wait until the end of the quarter. Export weekly or after each major assignment to keep PowerTeacher current.
    - **Verify after export**: Always spot-check a few students in PowerTeacher Pro after exporting to make sure grades transferred correctly.
