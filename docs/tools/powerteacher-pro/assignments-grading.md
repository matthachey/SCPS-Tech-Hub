# Creating Assignments and Entering Grades

Assignments are the graded items that live inside your categories. This guide covers creating assignments, entering scores, and using the Score Inspector.

**Time to complete:** ~8 min

---

## Creating an assignment

1. Click **"+ Create"** (top right) → **"Assignment"**.
2. Fill in the details:

| Field | What to Enter |
|-------|---------------|
| **Name** | Descriptive name (keep it under 30 characters if you use Canvas GradeSync) |
| **Category** | Select the appropriate district category |
| **Score Type** | Points, Percentage, or Letter Grade |
| **Points Possible** | Maximum points for the assignment |
| **Due Date** | When the assignment is due |
| **Include in Final Grade** | Check this unless it's a practice/ungraded assignment |

3. Click **Save**.

### Publishing options

On the **Publish** tab, choose when the assignment appears on the parent/student portal:

- **Days before due date** — Shows the assignment a set number of days early
- **On due date** — Only visible on the due date
- **Never** — Hidden from the portal (useful for internal tracking like benchmark assessments)

---

## Entering grades

### From the Assignments page

1. Click **Grading** → **Assignments**.
2. Click on an assignment name.
3. Student names appear with score entry fields.
4. Click in the score field next to a student's name and type the grade.
5. Click **Save**.

### Using the Score Inspector

When you click in a score cell, the **Score Inspector** panel appears on the right:

- Enter the **numeric score** using the keypad
- Add **score flags**: Late, Missing, Absent, Exempt, Collected, Incomplete
- Add a **comment** about the student's score
- Apply **special codes** if your school uses them (NC for no credit, etc.)

### Quick Fill

Need to give the whole class the same score?

1. Enter the score for one student.
2. Click the **Fill** icon (down arrow) in the Score Inspector.
3. Choose to fill **all empty fields** with that score.

!!! tip "Mark exceptions first, then Quick Fill"
    Mark any students who are absent, exempt, or missing first. Then use Quick Fill to enter the default score for everyone else. This prevents accidentally overwriting special statuses.

---

## Score flags explained

| Flag | What It Means | How It Affects the Grade |
|------|---------------|-------------------------|
| **Late** | Submitted past the due date | Score still counts (unless your school has a late policy) |
| **Missing** | Not submitted | Counts as a zero unless changed |
| **Absent** | Student was absent | Does not count in the grade calculation |
| **Exempt** | Excused from the assignment | Does not count in the grade calculation |
| **Collected** | Received but not yet graded | No impact until a score is entered |
| **Incomplete** | Started but not finished | Depends on school policy |
