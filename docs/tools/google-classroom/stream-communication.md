# Stream, Announcements, and Comments

Google Classroom offers several ways to communicate with students. The Stream is your class activity feed, and you can control how students interact with it.

**Time to complete:** ~5 min

---

## The Stream

The Stream is the first tab students see when they open your class. It shows recent activity: new assignments, announcements, and comments.

### Posting an announcement

1. At the top of the Stream, click **"Announce something to your class"** (or **"+"** on mobile).
2. Type your message.
3. Optionally **attach** files, links, Drive documents, or YouTube videos.
4. Click **"Post"** to send immediately, or click the dropdown to **schedule** it for later.

Announcements are great for reminders, updates, and sharing resources that don't need a grade.

---

## Controlling Stream permissions

In **Class Settings** (gear icon), you can control who can post:

| Setting | What It Means |
|---------|---------------|
| **Students can post and comment** | Full open discussion — can get noisy |
| **Students can only comment** | Students can reply to your posts but can't create their own |
| **Only teachers can post or comment** | Clean, teacher-controlled feed |

!!! tip "Recommended for most K-12 classes"
    **"Students can only comment"** is usually the sweet spot. You control the conversation topics, but students can still ask questions and engage with your posts.

---

## Private comments vs. Stream comments

| Type | Who Sees It | Where to Use It |
|------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Stream comments** | Everyone in the class | General questions, class discussions |
| **Private comments** | Only you and the student | Individual feedback, grade questions, personal matters |

Students can leave private comments on assignments. You can reply privately from the Student Work page.

---

## Emailing students and guardians

From the **People** tab:

1. Check the box next to a student's name (or select multiple).
2. Click **"Actions"** → **"Email"**.
3. This opens your Gmail with the selected recipients pre-filled.

For guardians, you can invite them to receive **guardian email summaries** — automatic weekly or daily digests of their student's upcoming and missing work.
