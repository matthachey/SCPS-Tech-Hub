# Navigating Google Classroom

Google Classroom is organized into four main tabs per class. Understanding each one will help you work efficiently.

**Time to complete:** ~5 min

---

## Accessing Google Classroom

1. Go to **[classroom.google.com](https://classroom.google.com)** or click the Classroom icon in your Google Apps waffle menu.
2. Sign in with your **SCPS Google account**.
3. You'll see your class cards on the homepage.

---

## The four tabs

When you open a class, you'll see four tabs across the top:

### Stream

The Stream is the activity feed — like a social media timeline for your class. Announcements appear here, and students see notifications when new assignments are posted. You can post quick updates, but the Stream is **not** the best place to organize classwork.

### Classwork

The Classwork tab is where you create and organize all assignments, quizzes, questions, and materials. This is the tab students should use to find their work. Content is organized by **topics** (your categories).

### People

The People tab shows your class roster — students and any co-teachers. From here you can:

- Invite or remove students
- Email students or guardians
- Mute individual students from posting on the Stream

### Grades

The Grades tab is your gradebook. It shows all graded assignments as columns and students as rows. You can enter, edit, and return grades directly from this view.

---

## Class card customization

On the homepage, each class appears as a card. You can:

- **Change the header image** — click the class card, then click **"Customize"** at the bottom right of the banner
- **Reorder classes** — drag and drop cards to arrange them by period
