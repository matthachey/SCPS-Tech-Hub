# Using the Grades Page (Gradebook)

The Grades page gives you a spreadsheet view of all student grades across all assignments. You can enter grades, return work, and track missing assignments from here.

**Time to complete:** ~5 min

---

## Accessing the Grades page

1. Open your class.
2. Click the **Grades** tab at the top.

---

## Understanding the layout

- **Rows** = Students
- **Columns** = Assignments (in the order they were created)
- **Overall grade** = Shown on the far left (if you've set up a grading system)
- **Color coding**: Red text = missing, Green text = turned in or draft grade

---

## Entering grades directly

1. Click any **empty grade cell**.
2. Type the score.
3. Press **Enter** — the grade saves as a draft.
4. To **return** the grade to the student, click the three-dot menu on the cell → **"Return"**.

---

## Sorting and filtering

- Click a **column header** to sort by that assignment's grades.
- Use the **grading period filter** at the top if you've set up grading periods (quarters, semesters).
- The overall grade column updates in real time as you enter grades.

---

## Exporting grades

1. On the Grades page, click the **gear icon** (Settings).
2. Click **"Copy all grades to Google Sheets"** — this creates a Google Sheet with all current grades.
3. You can also download grades from individual assignments.

!!! tip "Export at the end of each grading period"
    Create a Google Sheet snapshot of your grades before the end of each quarter. This gives you a backup and a record you can share with your administration if needed.
