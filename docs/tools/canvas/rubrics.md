# Using Rubrics

Rubrics in Canvas provide clear grading criteria for students and make your grading faster and more consistent. You can attach rubrics to assignments, discussions, and quizzes.

**Time to complete:** ~8 minutes

---

## Creating a rubric

1. Open an **assignment**, **graded discussion**, or **quiz**.
2. Click **"+ Rubric"** at the bottom of the page.
3. Build your rubric:

    - **Criteria** (rows): What you're evaluating (e.g., "Thesis Statement," "Evidence," "Grammar")
    - **Ratings** (columns): Performance levels (e.g., "Excellent," "Proficient," "Developing," "Beginning")
    - **Points**: Assign point values to each rating level

4. Click **"Create Rubric"** to save and attach it.

### Example rubric structure

| Criteria | Excellent (4) | Proficient (3) | Developing (2) | Beginning (1) |
|----------|---------------|-----------------|-----------------|----------------|
| Thesis Statement | Clear, arguable thesis that addresses the prompt | Thesis present and addresses the prompt | Thesis is vague or partially addresses the prompt | No clear thesis |
| Use of Evidence | 3+ relevant pieces of evidence with analysis | 2 pieces of evidence with some analysis | 1 piece of evidence, minimal analysis | No evidence cited |
| Organization | Logical flow with clear transitions | Generally organized with some transitions | Some organization but hard to follow | No clear organization |

---

## Rubric options

When creating or editing a rubric, you'll see these options:

| Option | What It Does |
|--------|--------------|
| **Use this rubric for assignment grading** | The rubric score becomes the assignment grade automatically |
| **Hide score total from students** | Students see individual criteria feedback but not the total — useful for feedback-only rubrics |
| **Free-form comments** | Lets you write custom comments for each criterion instead of selecting a pre-set rating |

!!! tip "Use rubric for grading"
    Check **"Use this rubric for assignment grading"** to make grading a one-click process. Click the rating level for each criterion, and Canvas calculates the total automatically.

---

## Reusing rubrics

Once you create a rubric, you can reuse it across assignments:

1. When adding a rubric to a new assignment, click **"Find a Rubric"**.
2. Search your course rubrics or browse by course.
3. Select the rubric and attach it.

You can also modify a copy of an existing rubric without changing the original.

---

## Grading with rubrics in SpeedGrader

1. Open **SpeedGrader** for the assignment.
2. The rubric appears on the right side.
3. **Click a rating level** for each criterion.
4. Add optional comments for each criterion.
5. Click **"Save"** — the total score populates automatically.

This makes grading an essay or project dramatically faster than writing individual feedback from scratch every time.
