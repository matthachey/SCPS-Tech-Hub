# Setting Up Your Course Homepage

The homepage is the first thing students see when they enter your course. A well-designed homepage reduces confusion and helps students find what they need immediately.

**Time to complete:** ~8 minutes

---

## Choosing a homepage layout

Canvas offers several homepage options. Go to your course **Home** page, then click **"Choose Home Page"** (on the right side):

| Layout | Best For |
|--------|----------|
| **Modules** | Most K-12 teachers. Students land directly on your organized content. |
| **Pages Front Page** | If you want a custom welcome page with links, images, and instructions. |
| **Assignment List** | Shows upcoming assignments. Less common in K-12. |
| **Syllabus** | Shows the syllabus and upcoming calendar items. |
| **Course Activity Stream** | Shows recent activity. Can be overwhelming for younger students. |

!!! tip "Recommended for K-12"
    **Modules** is the best default for most SCPS teachers. Students open the course and immediately see what they need to work on, organized in order. If you want a custom landing page with a welcome message and quick links, use **Pages Front Page** and link to your modules from there.

---

## Option A: Modules as your homepage

1. Go to **Home** → click **"Choose Home Page"**.
2. Select **"Course Modules"**.
3. Click **"Set as Course Home Page"**.

Students will now land on your Modules page when they enter the course. Make sure your modules are organized and the current one is at the top.

---

## Option B: Custom front page

1. Go to **Pages** in the course navigation.
2. Click **"+ Page"** to create a new page.
3. Design your homepage using the Rich Content Editor:
    - Add a **welcome message**
    - Include a **photo or banner**
    - Add **links to your current module**, class expectations, or key resources
    - Embed a **video introduction** if you want
4. Click **"Save"**.
5. On the saved page, click the **three dots** menu and select **"Use as Front Page"**.
6. Go to **Home** → **"Choose Home Page"** → select **"Pages Front Page"**.

!!! tip "Keep it simple"
    For younger students (middle school), a clean page with 3–4 big, clear links works best. Don't overload the homepage with too much information — think of it as a table of contents, not an encyclopedia.

---

## Publishing your course

Your course is not visible to students until you **publish** it.

1. Go to **Home**.
2. Click the **"Publish"** button (or use the dropdown menu on the Home or Settings page).
3. Students can now see and access the course.

!!! warning "Don't forget to publish"
    A common first-week issue: teachers set everything up but forget to publish the course. If students say they can't see your course, check this first.
