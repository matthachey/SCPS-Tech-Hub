# Announcements and Inbox

Canvas gives you two main communication tools: Announcements for broadcasting to your class, and the Inbox for direct messaging. This guide covers both.

**Time to complete:** ~5 minutes

---

## Announcements

Announcements are class-wide messages that appear at the top of your course and send notifications to students.

### Creating an announcement

1. Go to **Announcements** in the course navigation.
2. Click **"+ Announcement"**.
3. Add a **title** and write your message using the Rich Content Editor.
4. Optionally:
    - **Attach a file** or embed media
    - **Delay posting** — schedule it for a future date and time
    - **Allow users to comment** — let students respond to the announcement
5. Click **"Save"** to post immediately (or it will post at your scheduled time).

### When to use announcements

| Scenario | Example |
|----------|---------|
| Weekly overview | "This week we're working on Unit 4. Here's what to expect..." |
| Due date reminder | "Reminder: Your essay is due Friday at 11:59 PM." |
| Class update | "Great job on yesterday's discussion! Here are some highlights..." |
| Resource sharing | "I found this video that explains the concept really well — check it out." |

!!! tip "Post weekly announcements"
    A short Monday morning announcement outlining the week's plan keeps students on track and reduces "what are we doing?" questions. It takes 2 minutes to write and saves you countless interruptions.

## Canvas Inbox

The Inbox is Canvas's internal messaging system. It works like email but stays within Canvas.

### Sending a message

1. Click the **Inbox** icon in the global navigation (left sidebar).
2. Click the **compose icon** (pencil/paper icon).
3. Select the **course** from the dropdown.
4. Add recipients:
    - Type a student's name to search
    - Select **"All in [Course Name]"** to message the whole class
    - Select a specific **section** or **group**
5. Add a **subject** and write your message.
6. Click **"Send"**.

### Sending messages from the Gradebook

One of the most powerful Inbox features is messaging students based on grade criteria:

1. In the **Gradebook**, click the **three dots** on an assignment column.
2. Select **"Message Students Who..."**
3. Choose a criteria:
    - **Haven't submitted yet**
    - **Haven't been graded**
    - **Scored more than [X]**
    - **Scored less than [X]**
4. Canvas auto-populates the recipient list. Write your message and send.

!!! tip "Targeted messages save time"
    Instead of sending a blanket "turn in your work" message to everyone, use "Message Students Who haven't submitted yet." Students who already turned in work won't get an unnecessary reminder.

## Communication best practices

!!! tip "Tips from your ITRT"
    - **Use Announcements for the whole class**, Inbox for individual or small group messages.
    - **Keep Announcements short** — students skim. Put the most important info in the first sentence.
    - **Check your Inbox regularly** — students may message you with questions, especially outside of class.
    - **Encourage parents to use the Canvas Parent app** — it gives families visibility into announcements, grades, and upcoming assignments without needing to message you.
    - **Don't use Inbox for urgent issues** — Canvas Inbox is not real-time. For urgent matters, use email or phone.
