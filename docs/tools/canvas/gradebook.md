# Managing the Gradebook

The Canvas Gradebook is where you view, manage, and organize all student grades. It calculates final grades automatically based on your assignment groups and weighting.

**Time to complete:** ~10 minutes

---

## Accessing the Gradebook

Go to **Grades** in the course navigation. You'll see a spreadsheet-style view with students as rows and assignments as columns.

---

## Entering grades directly

You don't have to use SpeedGrader for everything. For simple grades:

1. **Click on any cell** in the Gradebook.
2. Type the score.
3. Press **Enter** or **Tab** to save and move to the next student.

This is fast for entering participation points, in-class activity grades, or attendance-based scores.

---

## Understanding the Gradebook display

### Column headers

Each column represents a graded item. Click the **column header** to see options:

- **Sort** by score (high to low, low to high)
- **Message students who** scored above/below a threshold, haven't submitted, etc.
- **Set default grade** — assign the same score to all ungraded students
- **Post/hide grades** — control when students see their scores
- **Open in SpeedGrader** — jump to grading for that assignment

### Status indicators

| Indicator | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|
| **Orange dot** | Late submission |
| **Blue dot** | Resubmitted (student submitted again after grading) |
| **Dash (—)** | No submission |
| **Grayed out cell** | Assignment not assigned to this student |
| **Excused** | Student is excused from this assignment |

### Marking an assignment as excused

1. Click the cell for the student/assignment.
2. Type **"EX"** and press Enter.
3. The assignment is marked as excused and won't count against the student's grade.

## Filtering and organizing

### Filter by section

If you teach multiple sections in one course, use the **section filter** at the top of the Gradebook to view one section at a time.

### Filter by assignment group

Click the **"View"** menu → **"Arrange by"** → select an assignment group to focus on just that category.

### Search for a student

Use the **search bar** at the top to quickly find a specific student.

---

## Assignment groups and weighting

If you've set up assignment group weighting (see [Creating an assignment](creating-assignments.md)), the Gradebook will show **group totals** and a **final grade** that reflects your weighting scheme.

The **Total** column on the far right shows each student's calculated course grade.

!!! warning "The Total column is powerful"
    This is the number that matters. Make sure your weighting is correct by checking a few students' grades manually. If the Total doesn't match what you expect, double-check your assignment group weights.

---

## Late policies

Canvas can automatically apply late penalties:

1. Click the **gear icon** (⚙) in the Gradebook.
2. Under **"Late Policies"**:
    - **Automatically apply grade for missing submissions**: Set a default score (like 0%) for anything past due with no submission.
    - **Automatically apply late submission deduction**: Set a percentage to deduct per day late (e.g., 10% per day, up to a maximum deduction).

!!! info "Check your school's late work policy"
    Before configuring automatic late penalties, make sure they align with your building or division's grading policy.

---

## Exporting grades

Need grades in a spreadsheet?

1. Click **"Export"** in the Gradebook (top right).
2. Select **"Export Entire Gradebook"**.
3. A CSV file downloads that you can open in Excel or Google Sheets.

This is useful for report cards, parent conferences, or data analysis.
