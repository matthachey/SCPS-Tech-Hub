# Creating an Assignment

Assignments are the most common graded item in Canvas. This guide covers creating assignments with different submission types, due dates, and point values.

**Time to complete:** ~8 minutes

---

## Step 1: Start a new assignment

There are two ways to create an assignment:

=== "From the Assignments page"
    1. Go to **Assignments** in the course navigation.
    2. Click **"+ Assignment"**.

=== "From within a module"
    1. Go to **Modules**, click the **"+"** on the module where you want the assignment.
    2. Select **"Assignment"** → **"New Assignment"**.

---

## Step 2: Set up the basics

| Field | What to Enter |
|-------|---------------|
| **Assignment Name** | Clear, descriptive name (e.g., `Unit 3 Essay: Causes of the Revolution`) |
| **Description** | Use the Rich Content Editor to write instructions, embed images/videos, and add links |
| **Points** | How many points the assignment is worth |
| **Assignment Group** | Which category it belongs to (e.g., Homework, Classwork, Assessments) |
| **Display Grade As** | Points, percentage, complete/incomplete, or letter grade |

!!! tip "Write clear descriptions"
    Include everything a student needs: what to do, how to do it, what format to submit, and what success looks like. Students should be able to complete the assignment from the description alone without asking you a question.

---

## Step 3: Choose a submission type

| Submission Type | What It Means | Best For |
|-----------------|---------------|----------|
| **Online** | Students submit digitally through Canvas | Most assignments |
| **On Paper** | No digital submission — you grade in person | Handwritten work, physical projects |
| **No Submission** | No student submission expected | Participation grades, in-class activities |
| **External Tool** | Students use a third-party tool | MasteryConnect, Turnitin, etc. |

### Online submission options

If you select **Online**, you'll see additional checkboxes:

- **Text Entry** — Students type directly in Canvas
- **File Uploads** — Students upload documents (Word, PDF, etc.)
- **Website URL** — Students submit a link
- **Media Recordings** — Students record audio or video
- **Student Annotation** — Students annotate a document you provide

!!! tip "For Google-heavy classrooms"
    If students do their work in Google Docs, have them submit as a **Website URL** (paste the sharing link) or use the **Google Assignments LTI** if it's configured in your SCPS Canvas account.

---

## Step 4: Set due dates and availability

| Field | What It Controls |
|-------|------------------|
| **Due Date** | When the assignment is due — submissions after this are marked late |
| **Available From** | When students can first see and access the assignment |
| **Until** | The last date students can submit (even late) |

!!! info "Due date vs. availability"
    The **due date** is the deadline. The **Until** date is when the submission window closes entirely. If you leave "Until" blank, students can submit late indefinitely (it will still be marked late).

### Differentiated due dates

Need different due dates for different students or sections?

1. In the **Assign** section, click **"+ Add"**.
2. Search for a specific student, section, or group.
3. Set unique due dates for each.

This is great for accommodations, makeups, or multi-section courses.

---

## Step 5: Save and publish

- Click **"Save"** to save as a draft (only you can see it).
- Click **"Save & Publish"** to make it visible to students immediately.

You can also publish later from the Assignments page or Modules page.

---

## Assignment groups and weighting

Assignments are organized into **Assignment Groups** (categories like Homework, Classwork, Tests). These groups can be weighted to calculate final grades.

### Setting up weighted grades

1. Go to **Assignments**.
2. Click the **three dots** menu in the top right.
3. Select **"Assignment Groups Weight"**.
4. Check **"Weight final grade based on assignment groups"**.
5. Enter the weight for each group (should total 100%).

| Group | Weight |
|-------|--------|
| Classwork | 30% |
| Homework | 20% |
| Assessments | 40% |
| Participation | 10% |

!!! warning "Check your division's grading policy"
    SCPS may have specific grading weight requirements. Check with your building administration before setting up your own weighting scheme.
