# Using SpeedGrader

SpeedGrader is Canvas's built-in grading tool that lets you view student submissions, provide feedback, use rubrics, and assign scores — all in one streamlined interface.

**Time to complete:** ~8 minutes

---

## Accessing SpeedGrader

There are several ways to get to SpeedGrader:

- From an **assignment page**: Click **"SpeedGrader"** in the right sidebar
- From the **Gradebook**: Click on a cell, then click the **SpeedGrader icon** (or select "Open in SpeedGrader")
- From **Modules**: Click the three dots on an assignment → **"SpeedGrader"**

---

## The SpeedGrader layout

SpeedGrader has three main areas:

**Left side**: Student list — navigate between students using the arrows or the dropdown

**Center**: The student's submission — you'll see their document, text entry, URL, or file

**Right side**: Grading tools — score field, rubric, and comments

---

## Grading a submission

### Assigning a score

1. Enter the score in the **grade field** (top right).
2. Press **Enter** or **Tab** to save.
3. Move to the next student with the **arrow buttons**.

### Using a rubric

If a rubric is attached:

1. Click **"View Rubric"** on the right side.
2. Click the appropriate **rating level** for each criterion.
3. Add optional comments per criterion.
4. Click **"Save Rubric"** — the total score auto-populates.

### Adding comments

1. Type feedback in the **comments box** on the right.
2. You can also leave **audio or video comments** — click the media icon.
3. Click **"Submit"** to save the comment.

!!! tip "Audio/video feedback"
    Recording a quick 30-second audio comment is often more meaningful than typed feedback and takes less time. Students appreciate hearing your voice, especially for complex assignments.

---

## Annotating submissions

For file uploads (Word docs, PDFs), you can annotate directly on the document:

1. Use the **annotation toolbar** that appears above the submission.
2. Tools include:
    - **Highlight** text
    - **Strikethrough** text
    - Add **point comments** (click anywhere to leave a note)
    - **Free draw** on the document
    - Add **text boxes**
3. Annotations save automatically and are visible to the student.

---

## Navigating between students

- Use the **left/right arrows** at the top to move between students
- Use the **dropdown menu** to jump to a specific student
- Sort students by name, submission date, or grade using the **settings** icon
- Filter to show only students who **need grading**

!!! tip "Filter for efficiency"
    Use the filter to show only **ungraded submissions**. This way you're not scrolling past students you've already graded.

---

## Posting grades

By default in many Canvas setups, grades may be **hidden** until you post them. This means students can't see their scores until you're ready.

1. After grading, go to the **Gradebook**.
2. Click the **three dots** on the assignment column.
3. Select **"Post Grades"**.
4. Choose to post for **everyone** or only **graded students**.

!!! info "Check your posting policy"
    Go to the Gradebook → assignment column → three dots → **"Grade Posting Policy"** to choose between **Automatic** (grades visible immediately) or **Manual** (you control when grades are visible).
