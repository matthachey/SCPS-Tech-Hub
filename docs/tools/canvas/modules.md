# Organizing Content with Modules

Modules are the backbone of your Canvas course. They let you organize content in a logical sequence — by unit, week, topic, or however you structure your instruction. Students work through modules in order, and you control what they see and when.

**Time to complete:** ~10 minutes

---

## What is a module?

A module is a container that holds a group of related items: pages, assignments, quizzes, discussions, files, and links. Think of it like a folder with a built-in sequence.

A typical module might look like:

> **Unit 3: The American Revolution**
>
> - :material-file-document: Overview and Learning Goals (Page)
> - :material-link: Primary Source: Declaration of Independence (Link)
> - :material-clipboard-text: Reading Response (Assignment)
> - :material-help-circle: Unit 3 Quiz (Quiz)
> - :material-forum: Discussion: Was the Revolution inevitable? (Discussion)

---

## Creating a module

1. Go to **Modules** in the course navigation.
2. Click **"+ Module"** in the top right.
3. Give the module a **name** (e.g., `Unit 3: The American Revolution` or `Week of Jan 13`).
4. Optionally set a **lock date** (students can't access until that date).
5. Click **"Add Module"**.

---

## Adding items to a module

1. Click the **"+"** button at the top of the module.
2. Choose what to add:

    | Item Type | When to Use |
    |-----------|-------------|
    | **Assignment** | Select an existing assignment or create a new one |
    | **Quiz** | Select an existing quiz |
    | **File** | Upload or link to a document, PDF, or image |
    | **Page** | A Canvas page with text, images, and embedded content |
    | **Discussion** | A discussion board |
    | **External URL** | A link to a website |
    | **External Tool** | A third-party tool (like MasteryConnect, Diffit, etc.) |
    | **Text Header** | A section divider within the module (great for organizing) |

3. Click **"Add Item"**.

!!! tip "Use text headers"
    Text headers help break up large modules. For example, within a unit module, you might have headers for "Readings," "Activities," and "Assessment." This makes it easier for students to scan and find what they need.

---

## Reordering items and modules

- **Drag and drop** items within a module to reorder them.
- **Drag and drop** entire modules to change the module order.
- The current/active module should always be at the **top** (or clearly marked).

---

## Publishing modules and items

Modules and individual items have separate publish states:

- A **green checkmark** means the item is published (visible to students)
- A **gray circle** means it's unpublished (hidden from students)

To publish or unpublish:

1. Click the **publish icon** (cloud/checkmark) next to the module name to publish the entire module and all its items.
2. Click the publish icon next to an **individual item** to publish just that item.

!!! warning "Publish strategically"
    Only publish the current module and anything students need right now. Keep future modules unpublished. This prevents students from working ahead before you're ready, and keeps their view clean.

---

## Setting module requirements

You can require students to complete items in order before moving to the next:

1. Click the **three dots** on the module and select **"Edit"**.
2. Under **"Requirements"**, add completion criteria:
    - **View the item** — student must open it
    - **Mark as done** — student must click "Mark as Done"
    - **Submit the assignment** — student must submit work
    - **Score at least X** — student must reach a minimum score
3. Optionally check **"Students must complete requirements in order"** for sequential progression.

!!! info "Use requirements sparingly in K-12"
    Sequential requirements work well for self-paced content but can frustrate students if they get stuck on one item and can't access anything else. Consider using requirements for critical items only.

---

## Module best practices

!!! tip "Tips from your ITRT"
    - **Name modules clearly**: `Unit 3: The American Revolution` is better than `Module 3`.
    - **One module per unit or week**: Don't create a module for every single day — it clutters the view.
    - **Put the current module on top**: Students should see what's relevant NOW first.
    - **Include a "Start Here" module**: At the top of your course, create a module with your syllabus, expectations, and a course orientation. It stays there all year.
    - **Use Pages for content, not Files**: A Canvas Page with embedded content looks better and is more accessible than asking students to download a file.
