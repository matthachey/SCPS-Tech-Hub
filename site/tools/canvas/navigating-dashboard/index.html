    Navigating the Canvas Dashboard¶

    The Canvas dashboard is your starting point every time you log in. This guide walks you through the main areas so you can find what you need quickly.

    Time to complete: ~5 minutes

    Logging in¶

    1. Go to your SCPS Canvas URL (bookmarked on your school devices).
    2. Sign in with your SCPS credentials (Google SSO or direct login, depending on your building's setup).

    The Dashboard¶

    When you log in, the Dashboard shows your active courses as cards. Each card displays the course name, section, and a color you can customize.

    What you can do from the Dashboard:

    • Click a course card to enter that course
    • View your To Do list on the right sidebar — upcoming items to grade
    • See recent activity across all courses
    • Use the Calendar (global navigation) to see all due dates across courses

    Customize your cards

    Click the three dots on any course card to nickname the course or change its color. Use colors that match your period schedule for quick visual identification.

    Global Navigation (left sidebar)¶

    The left sidebar is always available no matter where you are in Canvas:

    Icon What It Does
    Dashboard Returns you to your course card view
    Courses Lists all your current and past courses
    Calendar Shows all due dates, events, and assignments across courses
    Inbox Canvas messaging — send/receive messages to students, parents, and colleagues
    History Recently visited pages across all courses
    Commons Shared resource library — find and share course content

    Course Navigation (inside a course)¶

    Once you click into a course, the left sidebar changes to show that course's navigation:

    Menu Item What It's For
    Home The course landing page students see first
    Announcements Post class-wide messages
    Modules Your organized course content (the main student-facing view)
    Assignments All graded items — assignments, quizzes, discussions
    Grades The Gradebook
    People Your class roster
    Pages Content pages you've created
    Files Uploaded documents and media
    Discussions Discussion boards
    Quizzes Quiz management

    Clean up your sidebar

    Go to Settings → Navigation to hide menu items students don't need. A cleaner sidebar means less confusion. Most K-12 teachers keep Home, Modules, Assignments, Grades, and Announcements visible and hide the rest.

    Student View¶

    Want to see what your course looks like from a student's perspective?

    1. Go to Settings (bottom of the course navigation).
    2. Click "Student View" on the right side.
    3. You'll see the course exactly as a student would.
    4. Click "Leave Student View" (top of the page) to return to your teacher view.

    Test everything in Student View

    Before launching a new assignment or module, always check Student View. It's the fastest way to catch broken links, hidden items, or confusing navigation.
